Day 1: GX Coin Protocol – Vision Clarification & EERD Conceptual Design
Objective
 To establish a unified conceptual foundation for the GX Coin Protocol by clarifying its core vision, refining the conceptual entity-relationship model, and validating the design against the existing Hyperledger Fabric chaincode implementation.

Vision & Concept Clarification
 Work Carried Out: Began the session by analyzing the whitepaper and project documentation to define the protocol’s unique identity as a Productivity-Based Currency, clearly distinguishing it from both stablecoins and speculative cryptocurrencies. Developed a shared vocabulary for key terms such as protocol vs application layer and calibration vs pegging, ensuring conceptual precision for all subsequent architecture decisions.
 Lessons Learned: Establishing a clear conceptual and linguistic foundation is critical in complex systems design. Agreeing on terminology early prevents ambiguity and ensures alignment between technical, economic, and governance domains throughout development.

Enhanced Entity-Relationship Diagram (EERD) – Conceptual Design
 Work Carried Out: Conducted a structured identification of all logical system entities, dividing them into on-chain and off-chain categories. Grouped entities into thematic layers such as Identity, Accounts, and Governance. Introduced a Generalization/Specialization pattern for the on_Account entity to capture system funds (treasuries, loan pools, social benefit funds) as specialized sub-types.
 Lessons Learned: Adopting a layered, modular approach to entity modeling makes complex ecosystems manageable. Evaluating whether entities like on_SocialBenefitFundAccount warranted specialization versus independent tables deepened understanding of scalability, data integrity, and future adaptability.

Chaincode Analysis & Model Validation
 Work Carried Out: Reviewed the current Hyperledger Fabric chaincode implementation in Go—specifically types.go, identity_contract.go, tokenomics_contract.go, and organization_contract.go. Mapped the physical constructs (DocType fields, embedded structs, key-value pairs) back to the conceptual EERD to ensure logical consistency.
 Lessons Learned: Conceptual models and physical implementations serve complementary purposes. Understanding Fabric’s denormalized storage model highlighted how performance optimizations can coexist with sound conceptual integrity. This validation step confirmed that the proposed EERD accurately reflects the on-chain logic while preserving efficiency.

Outcomes
Unified conceptual understanding of GX Coin’s mission and architectural scope.


Completed a high-level EERD mapping all major entities with specialization hierarchy.


Validated conceptual model against real chaincode structures to ensure consistency.


Established terminology and modeling conventions for future backend and documentation work.
