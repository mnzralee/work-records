Date: Monday, October 27, 2025 
Title: Production Network Stabilization, TLS Hardening, and Critical Resource Exhaustion Diagnosis
Objective: To conduct a full-day troubleshooting session to stabilize the production network. The goals were to correct severe configuration errors, enable Admin TLS for the ordering service, join all peers to the application channel, and diagnose the root cause of persistent CouchDB container crashes.
Work Carried Out:
Initial Assessment & Configuration Triage: The day began with a deep review of all 12 protocol documents and a production readiness assessment, which identified 45 critical issues. A diagnosis of the running network confirmed 5 orderers were active, but 3 of 4 peers were up and 2 of 4 CouchDB containers were crashed. Critically, all osnadmin commands were failing because the Orderer Admin TLS was disabled.
YAML Configuration Hardening: The first action was to correct multiple, severe errors in the docker-compose-production.yaml and config/orderer.yaml files.
In docker-compose-production.yaml, Admin TLS was enabled for all 5 orderer services with the proper certificate paths, duplicate network entries and malformed environment variables were fixed, and restart: unless-stopped policies were added for service stability.
In config/orderer.yaml, the configuration was updated from Admin.TLS.Enabled: false to true, and the ListenAddress was changed from 127.0.0.1:9443 to 0.0.0.0:9443 to allow access from other Docker containers.
Network Restart & Channel Verification: With the configurations corrected, the entire network was cleanly restarted. The CouchDB instances were started first, followed by the orderers and peers. The newly enabled Admin TLS was successful, and the osnadmin tool confirmed that all 5 orderers had already joined the gxchannel (data persisted from a previous session), establishing a healthy Raft consensus cluster.
Peer Joining & Troubleshooting: Attention then turned to joining the peers. peer0.org1 and peer1.org1 were successfully joined to the channel. This required resolving a TLS/hostname issue: the default localhost address was causing an IPv6 resolution error, which was fixed by using hostname-based addressing and adding the peer hostnames to the server's /etc/hosts file for proper TLS certificate validation.
Critical Resource Exhaustion & OOM Diagnosis: The attempt to join peer0.org2 failed, as its ledger was stuck in an UNDER_CONSTRUCTION state. The root cause was traced to its database, couchdb2, which was in an OOM (Out of Memory) crash loop. An investigation into resource limits revealed that the VPS itself (7.5GB RAM, 4 CPU) was completely exhausted, running at 100% CPU and 100% memory load. Attempts to apply mem_limit: 512m to the CouchDB containers failed, as research confirmed the service requires more than 512MB just to initialize.
Strategic Decision & Next Steps: Removing all memory limits allowed 3 of 4 CouchDBs to stabilize, but the system remained critically overloaded, blocking any further progress (like chaincode deployment). Based on this definitive diagnosis, the decision was made to upgrade the VPS server. A meeting was scheduled for the next day to discuss the new server specifications and a potential re-architecture to overcome this resource bottleneck.
Outcome: The session was a partial success. The network's configuration is now correct, and the critical Admin TLS service is enabled and functional. A 5-node Raft consensus cluster is active, and 2 of 4 peers are successfully joined to the channel. However, a critical blocker was identified: the VPS is 100% resource-exhausted, causing catastrophic OOM crashes in the CouchDB containers. Chaincode deployment is blocked. The immediate next step is to upgrade the VPS resources and hold a strategic meeting to plan the new deployment architecture.
